Welcome to Golf Instructions

This page explains the important stuff about playing golf, from what you should do when you arrive at the course to the steps you need to take to have the most fun while playing!

Before you tee off, it helps to know a few basic golf terms!

What to do when you get to the golf course

When you get there, go check in at the pro shop, warm up at the driving range or practice green, and get ready to tee off on time!

Key Features on the Golf Course

The golf course has lots of areas like the tee box, fairway, rough, bunkers, and green. Knowing these places will help you plan your shots better and feel more confident when you play.

Golf Terms Every Beginner Should Know:

  • Fairway

    The short, neat grass between the tee box and the green.

  • Rough

    Thicker, longer grass beside the fairway. Harder to play from.

  • Tee Box

    The flat spot where the first shot is taken at each hole.

  • Bunker

    A sand-filled pit. You'll need special technique to get out!

  • Green

    The area around the hole with very short grass, where you putt.

  • Handicap

    A number showing how good you are, used to make matches fairer.

  • Par

    The expected number of strokes to complete a hole or round.

  • Birdie, Eagle, Albatross

    Terms for scoring below par: Birdie = 1 under, Eagle = 2 under, Albatross = 3 under.

  • Bogey, Double Bogey, Triple Bogey

    Terms for going over par: 1, 2, or 3 extra strokes.
